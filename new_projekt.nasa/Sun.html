The Sun

The Sun is a star at the center of the Solar System, around which planets, asteroids, comets, and other celestial bodies orbit. It is the primary source of light and heat for Earth.

The Sun is composed mainly of hydrogen (about 74%) and helium (about 24%), with trace amounts of heavier elements such as oxygen, carbon, neon, and iron.

Mercury

Mercury is the closest planet to the Sun and the smallest planet in the Solar System. It has a very thin atmosphere, which means it cannot retain heat, resulting in extreme temperature fluctuations.

The surface of Mercury is heavily cratered and resembles that of the Moon. Due to its proximity to the Sun, it is difficult to observe Mercury from Earth.

Mercury has a core made of iron, and it is believed that a significant portion of its interior is molten. Despite its small size, Mercury has a significant magnetic field.
